Finding Inner Peace in a Negative World

Listen the blog here:

In today's noisy world, it can feel like we're constantly bombarded by negativity - from the news, social media, and even our own thoughts. It's easy to get swept up in this tide of pessimism, but there are ways we can transcend the negativity and find inner peace. As author Jay Shetty writes in his book "Think Like a Monk," purifying our own thoughts is the first line of defense against outside influences. Negative thoughts and words are like mosquitoes - even the smallest ones can rob us of peace. We have to monitor our self-talk and catch ourselves when we engage in what Shetty calls "the cancer of the mind" - comparing, complaining, and criticizing.

Shetty advises not giving attention to what others do, but instead focusing on our own actions. Once we recognize our own negative tendencies, we can reverse them through mindfulness, self-compassion, and conscious positive thinking. This takes practice, but gets easier over time. When we encounter negativity from others, we can neutralize it by not reacting. As Shetty explains, we think saying whatever we want is freedom, but real freedom is not feeling compelled to react. When we refrain from getting hooked by someone else's words or actions, we take back our power.

With social media, it helps to be selective about what we take in. If certain people or news feeds tend to generate negative emotions, we can unfollow or mute them. We can also balance this selective media consumption with accounts that inspire and uplift us. Finally, Shetty emphasizes the importance of self-care in creating positivity. Getting enough sleep, eating healthy foods, exercising, and making time for our hobbies all help elevate our mood and provide a buffer against stress. When we care for our whole self, it's easier to experience inner calm regardless of outer conditions. The more we master our state of mind, the less external negativity can disturb our equilibrium. By making peace with ourselves, we are able to dwell in a state of grace no matter what turbulence surrounds us. With consistency and compassion for ourselves and others, we become anchors of light in a fragmented world.What are your thoughts on cultivating inner peace?
